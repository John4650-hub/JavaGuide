# Introduction

**"Back-end Interview High Frequency System Design & Scenes"** is an internal book of my [Knowledge Planet](../about-the-author/zhishixingqiu-two-years.md) that contains common system design cases such as short-chain systems, strangulation systems, and high-frequency scenes such as big data re-engineering and third-party authorized login.

In recent years, with more and more technical interviews in the country, a growing number of companies have begun to look at system design and scenes in interviews as a way to assess job-seekers more fully, whether they are in school or in society. However, it is rare for regular interviews to be full of scenes, and the interviewers usually evaluate you through one or two system designs and scenes.

So I summarized this "Back-end Interview HF System Design & Scenes," which includes common system design cases such as short-chain systems, second-kill systems, and high-frequency features such as big data weighting and third-party authorized login.

Even if it is not for an interview, I strongly recommend that you read this series of articles carefully, which will be very helpful in enhancing your design thinking and problem-solving skills. Moreover, many of the cases involved can be applied in your own projects, such as the design of a lottery system and third-party authorized login, along with the right way that Redis achieves delayed tasks.

The "Back-end Interview HF System Design & Site Design," which is part of the Java Interview Point North, is mentioned separately because of its size.
