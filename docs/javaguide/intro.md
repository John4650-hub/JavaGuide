# Project Introduction

**Category:** Closer project\
**Icon:** about

I graduated from college in '19 and I opened JavaGuide when I was ready for an interview. I've shared some of my summaries in preparation for the interview without reservation through JavaGuide.

The original idea of JavaGuide stems from a rather confused personal learning experience, the main purpose of which is to help small partners who encounter problems in learning Java or interviewing.

- **For Java beginners:** This document tends to provide you with a more detailed learning path, a clear catalog, and a clear understanding of Java's overall knowledge system. You can follow a video or a book to learn a certain knowledge point, and then you can come here to find the corresponding summary, which will help you better understand the corresponding knowledge point.
- **For non-Java beginners:** This document is more appropriate for reviewing knowledge and preparing for interviews, so that you can figure out what should be the focus of the interviews.

**The quality of the content is high owing to the fact that it is fully open and well maintained by all.** If you also want to be involved in the maintenance of JavaGuide, see [Contribution-guideline.md].

JavaGuide means more to me than to any other friend who has learned something through JavaGuide or helped to get it. Not to exaggerate, sometimes it feels like my own child is growing up, and I've been protecting it with my heart. It took me a long time to keep it, but I think it's worth it! Very meaningful!

However, I hope you don't have any luck with the interview, but you have to fight hard! **I'd like this file to show you where Java is going, not just for interviews.**

Moreover, JavaGuide could not have covered all of the interviews, especially the more in-depth interviews such as Ali and the American Corps. You can learn from your target companies in a targeted and in-depth way, and you can look at the faces of some of the target companies, and you can see for yourself and think about it.

Come on! Ollie!
