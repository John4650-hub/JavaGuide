Foreword

A good curriculum vitae can play a very important role throughout the interview application and interview process.

**Why is the curriculum vitae important?** We can say the following:

**1. CV is like one of our faces, and it determines to a large extent the availability of interviews.**

- If you're online, your resume will certainly go through HR, and a resume HR will probably take about 10 seconds to look at and decide if you can get into the interview.
- If you're an insider, if your résumé has no advantage, you can't do anything about it.

Furthermore, even if you pass the first round of screenings and are given an interview, the interviewer will judge, on the basis of your resume, whether you are worth a lot of time.

The focus of the interviewer's questions is largely determined by the contents of the curriculum vitae. \*\*

- You usually ask questions about what you have on your résumés (Java Foundation, Gathering, Co-issue, MySQL, Redis, Spring, Spring Boot, which is everybody's question), like writing about your proficient use of Redis, and the interviewer has a great chance of asking you some questions about Redis, and you write about your use of news queues in the project.
- Skills also determine to a large extent the depth of the interviewer's questions.

Without exaggerating their abilities, writing a good curriculum vitae is also a great ability. In general, technical and learning skills are better, and curricula vitae are better written!

# Curricula Vitae Template

The CV style is really very important! If your resume style is so ugly that you don't have friends, the interviewer really doesn't want to see it. The pain of handling hundreds of résumés a day, you don't understand!

I suggest here that you write your curriculum vitae using the Markdown syntax and then translate the Markdown format into PDF format for your delivery. If you do not know much about the Markdown syntax, you can take half an hour to read the Markdown syntax note: <http://www.markdown.cn/>.

Here are some of the good CV templates I collected:

- Collection of curriculum vitae templates suitable for Chinese (recommended, open source free): <https://github.com/dyweb/awesome-resume-for-chinese>
- Wood and curriculum vitae (recommended, partially free of charge): <https://www.mujicv.com/>
- Brief curriculum vitae (recommended, partially free): <https://easycv.cn/>
- Brief curriculum vitae (free): <https://www.polebrief.com/index>
- Markdown Curriculum Tool (open source free): <https://reume.mdnice.com/>
- Director's curriculum vitae (fee, in support of AI generation): <https://jianli.chinaz.com/>
- Typola+markdown+cs Custom CV template: <https://github.com/Snailclimb/typola-markdown-resume>
- Super curricula vitae (partial fees): <https://www.worldercv.com/>

Most of the curriculum vitae templates above are one page only, and it is difficult to present enough information. If you're not a top-class candidate (for example, in the ACM competition), I suggest you write as much as possible to highlight your abilities (in 2 pages for admission to school, in 3 pages for admission to society, in 3 pages for refinement of language, and not too much nonsense).

**Reissued for technical reasons.**

- Try to be simple, not too fancy.
- Technical terms are better to specify case size, such as java -> Java, Spring Boot -> Spring Boot. While some interviewers will not mind this, many interviewers will care about the details.
- The addition of spaces between Chinese and digital English would seem more comfortable.
