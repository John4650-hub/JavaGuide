It's been six years since I've been writing technical blogs, and it's been a small milestone.

At first, I wrote a technical blog that simply summed up the subjects I studied in my classroom, such as the web and operating systems. Gradually, I started to write a more systematic summary of knowledge points and frequently asked questions.

[JavaGuide homepage](https://oss.javaguide.cn/about-the-author/college-live/image-202330081317766.png)

Many people want to write technical blogs, but it's not clear what it's for them. Some people start writing technical blogs without knowing how to keep going or what to write. I'm going to talk about some of my ideas and thoughts about recording technology blogs, which might help you solve these problems.

# What are the benefits of writing technology blogs?

## Better learning and better knowledge points

**Feynman Learning Method** You should know that this is a proven and effective way of learning. The name of the Feynman learning method comes from Richard Feynman, a physicist who received the Nobel Prize for Physics and who participated in the Manhattan Project.

The so-called Feynman learning method is that when you learn new knowledge, imagine yourself as a teacher: repeat and express complex and profound knowledge in the simplest and most straightforward words, preferably not using trade terms, so that people outside the industry can understand. In order to do that, it's best to imagine that you're teaching an 8-year-old child, even if they understand.

[Teaching others to learn is best](https://oss.javaguide.cn/about-the-author/college-live/v2-1973c2e6173c5083ee4b1d1523f8f5_720w.png)

Book and video readings are passive and less effective. Feynman learning methods are active and very effective.

**Writing a technical blog is actually a way to teach others.** However, when writing technical blogs, there are professional terms (unless your group is unskilled), but you need to express them in your own words so that others can see them as easily as possible. **Desperate to copy books or simply copy others' summaries!**

If we passively learn a certain point of knowledge, most of the time it may be just to satisfy the level of what we can use; you will not study its rationale, and even many key concepts are not understood.

If you're going to summarize the knowledge you've learned into a blog, it will deepen your thinking about this knowledge point. Many times, in order to make a point of knowledge clear, you go back and look at a lot of information, even a lot of source codes, and these tiny accumulations deepen your understanding of this point of knowledge in a substantial way.

Even I often find this in my blog: **I suddenly realize that my understanding of a knowledge point is wrong.**

**Blogging is itself a process of taking stock, reviewing, and thinking about the knowledge that you have learned. The blog is also a record of your learning experience. As time passes and age grows, how can this not be a valuable spiritual asset?**

# Helping others get a sense of achievement

Just like our programmers want their products to be recognized and liked, we write technology blogs in one way or another, of course, to get approval.

**When you write something that helps others, you have a sense of achievement and happiness.**

[Reader's approval](https://oss.javaguide.cn/about-the-author/college-live/image-20230441906257.png)

This sense of achievement and happiness will continue to inspire you to blog as **resulting**.

But even with the appreciation of many readers, there is too much to learn with humility. There are a lot of people out there who can read better than you.

Of course, you could get a lot of debate. There's probably a lot of people who say that you're writing without any depth, and there's probably a lot of people who say that you're having trouble with your writing, that you're writing on the Internet/book.

**Be honest with these arguments, be yourself, and walk your own way! Self-prove it!**
