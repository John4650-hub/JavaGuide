# The Text

In the social context of the rapid expansion of the Internet, there is a growing influx of IT workers from all walks of life into the Internet.

As early as 2016, our division published information on the recruitment of Java and iOS Engineers, receiving nearly 200 daily CVs for the individual positions of Java Engineers and nearly 1,000 for iOS.

That's right, that's what training institutions started on the market for iOS engineers. With the growth of recent years, however, the market ratio of supply to demand has increased. Personnel mapping has become a major problem for employers regarding talent appraisals.

The first time the author communicated with a job seeker in an interview was June 2015. Due to the short time and inexperience of the author at the time, some excellent job seekers were missed.

Three years later, today, the author has been able to communicate more with job seekers as a result of the expansion of the company.

## 1. How to Identify Poor Curriculum Vitae

In addition to providing technical training, training institutions often provide guidance on the preparation of curricula vitae and interviews. There are many creeping things we can't identify. However, the curriculum vitae packaged by the training institution has a uniform character.

**Young but Advanced Diploma**

The fact that young people have advanced diplomas may not be a criterion, but most candidates meet the market for traditional diplomas. Individual technology lovers may obtain diplomas through self-testing, which requires unique technological highlights.

**Older but Almost Without Technical Experience**

The problem is more serious for older candidates who have little technical experience. It is well known that a normal person's ability to accept new things decreases with age, and Internet technologies are included. If a person is older and does not have technical experience, there are only two scenarios:

1. Transit (forced entry through training, self-education, etc.).
1. Existing experience does not appear in the curriculum vitae due to capacity problems (compatibility of competencies with experience/payroll).

**Project Experience is Mostly in Management Systems**

Project experience, which is used to assess the level of candidates, is too appropriate. Every year, as the Internet evolves, there are many innovative Internet companies and new industries. The recruitment needs recently issued by the author included CRM systems, comptoirs, XX management systems, questionnaire systems, and classroom systems, which accounted for 90 percent. Now in 2019, is the internal management system so hot? To put it simply, our assessment of the curriculum vitae should give more thought to the item “do it.” For example, the person was employed in company XX, which did develop the project (except for outsourcing) in the context of that company.

**The Background of the Project Does Not Correspond to the Development of the Internet**

The background of the project varies from an earlier year's electrician, lottery wave to later O2O, poignant, live, and new retail. The emergence of each series of products is consistent with market definitions. In the case of the 18-year, 19-year-old lottery tickets (the 15-year-old government banned the Internet lottery), O2O, comptoirs, treasure-hunting products (the 17-year-old ban), and live broadcasting. It is clear that it is very incompatible with market demand. In such cases, it is necessary to consider whether there is room for understanding of the circumstances.

**Lack of Innovation**

Multiple project technical structures or project structures under different work experiences are consistent and lack innovation. In general, different firms differ in their technology portfolios and even in their product orientation and patterns. Thus, when a candidate is involved in multiple projects in many companies, the technology is uniform and business processes are identical. It seems neat, but it is even less convincing.
