# The Lead

I'm 36 years old, and I've had eight years of JAVA development experience. In Ariane, three and a half years, four and a half, are now standard senior programmers.

My values, my vision of life, and my worldview have also evolved over the years through reading, learning, and thinking. I realized that my interest was in creating an educational culture, and so in a semi-intense manner, in late August, I went out to get a job. Limited reasoning can't stop an impulsive personality. It should be planned and scientifically sound not to recommend nudity.

Although at first, I thought that I had the “ideal and purposeful will to be able” and that it should be easy to find an educational development position, in fact, I was too optimistic. Realism soon poured cold water on me. I have fought and lost. I'm surprised to find myself with this resilience. Interviews are an experience from which, if not defeated, provide a resilience that allows people to go further. Who hasn't experienced failure? Failure is the greatest mentor if you want to learn from it.

During the interview, I quickly discovered my disadvantages:

- Investing in business, with insufficient technical depth, and understanding of principles at a shallower level;
- Lack of a broad vision, limited to the line of order that they make, and insufficient knowledge of other related lines of business (e.g., commodities, marketing, payments, etc.);
- Inadequate thought, much of the time devoted to development and testing, and little reflection at the operational, product, business, and strategic levels;
- Lack of management experience and age; these two disadvantages, which I underestimated at one time, have come to the fore, even to the point where I was not confident, but I finally came out.

But I have my own advantages. The basic law of professional competition is scarcity and differentiation. It is a manifestation of scarcity that can solve the architecture and capture technical difficulties of large-scale projects, and of the scarcity of know-how in a high-end technology field; it is a manifestation of differences that can be well-articulated, with experience in high-mixed-flow system development. Scarcity is the best policy, differentiation is the medium, and reduction is the next.
