# Recommendation 1: Ideas to Strengthen Demand Assessment Capacity

Start with the needs assessment. In Internet companies, demand assessment is the main entry point for development.

For ordinary programmers, it is generally based on the details of the needs raised by the product manager that the idea of how this function is to be realized begins to be assumed, and the development costs may take some time. I think I'm a translator between needs and code. Little thought has been given to the legitimacy of the need and to the value of what you do.

For high-level programmers, it is not about getting into detail at the outset, but rather more from the product itself, asking the product manager why and for what. In other words, we'll consider whether this is a legitimate need.

PK is performed if the demand is not reasonable, either by adjusting the demand or cutting it off. But what needs to be noted is that the PK and the adjustment of demand are not just cutting demand; there is another direction, which is to strengthen demand.

Product students may not think enough because of a lack of technical background, and at this point, if you have a better idea, you can well add it to the demand and make it more valuable.

In any case, the senior programmer would not be able to follow up on the product manager's needs file in one place or another.

Such work may appear to have nothing to do with development, but only in that way would it be possible to ensure that all the students involved in the follow-up development were valuable, rather than doing a lot of useless work. The sense of achievement in development is deeply frustrated by the fact that more is done with no use.

As a result, **general programmers will have to strengthen their capacity to assess needs if they are to grow to a higher level of development.**

# Recommendation 2: Active Thinking of Efficiency

A regular programmer writes the code in order. I'll do it if I have a job. I'll stay here if I don't. There is little in-depth reflection on why these existing codes are written, what the benefits are, where there are bottlenecks, and whether I can optimize them.

The senior programmer, however, is not limited to the task of developing the master. They will take the initiative to figure out that this development model is not good enough. So can I do something that will increase this efficiency?

To give a small example, when I took over a project six years ago, I found out that the operation would come to me four times a month to send her a delivery. She said that's how it was done. Although this need is handled very simply, it will be released in two lines. But if you think you're concentrating on the code, she's come to you again, and she's cutting your mind off. And there is an inherent risk of uncertainty on the line of operations, and it's over when it's wrong.

My approach was that I spent a week making her a back office. Then all the operations were sent to her to work directly backstage. I'm going to do something more valuable.

So, **the second proposal is to think about where there is room for improvement in existing work and to think about it!**
