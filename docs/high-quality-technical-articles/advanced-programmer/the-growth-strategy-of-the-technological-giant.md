# 1. Preface

In the Bopo community of micro-credit technology exchanges, participants often ask questions about how technologists should learn to grow, and although it is a micro-credit exchange, I can still feel the anxiety of small partners.

**What are tech people worried about?** With all due respect, it's too small to be bold. Courage is courage, and people who are anxious are generally afraid of uncertainty about the future. People who know and are anxious generally cannot see the world around them, nor can they see themselves and the way they fit. Patterns are also aspirational, and people who are vulnerable to anxiety usually have a narrow vision. If viewed from a strategic and managerial perspective, there is a lack of awareness of themselves and the world around them, a lack of a clear and long-term learning and growth strategy, and of implementable milestones + rigorous implementation.

Because of the large number of participants asking such questions, which I am a little tired of, and in order to avoid repeating the answers, I have devoted myself to this long text and to trying to answer such questions in a uniform manner. If there is a similar question later, I will lead them to read the article and then give them three months, a year, or even more to think about and answer the question: **What is your technological growth strategy?** If you want this question to be clear, with clear and ready answers, then congratulations, you just have to do it according to sequence, without anxiety, and it is only a matter of time before you achieve your own strategic goals and achievements; otherwise, you still need to understand the big problems of this life by grinding and thinking!

Now, let's see how some of the technical bulls do it.

We know there's design models for software, but there's growth patterns for skilled people. Bobo often looks at the growth history of some of the technocrats on LinkedIn and explores the growth patterns in them, thus stimulating the development of his own technology growth strategy.

Of course, very few skilled bulls will give you a clear picture of their technological growth strategies and a detailed landing plan for each year. In fact, **the more powerful technologists, the clearer their technological growth strategies and paths, the easier we can find some successful models.**
