# Work

I didn't transfer to the inside of the e-mail, but I did a lot of things, including BUGLY, the distributional call chain (Huskie), the Soho, and the EPC measurement system. Some of them are external, some are internal, and some may not be known. I am also more grateful for the experience of these projects, both business-based and framework-based, which has enabled me to learn a lot.

Now, let's give you a quick introduction to each project, which, after all, has required a lot of effort:

BUGLY, this is an entry crash company reporting system, a lot of apps in use. Huskie, a distributed call chain tracking project based on Zipkin. SOHO, a crowd-sourcing system, has been used mainly to package data standards and voice-gathering tasks. EPC Measurement System, a research and development performance measurement system, primarily measuring R&D performance. I'm here to talk about my understanding of business development, and many people may be at the beginning of their careers, and there's a question as to how they grow while doing business all day. In other words, how do you grow while doing CRD all day? I started with this confusion, and then I changed my mind.

I believe that the complexity of the system can be broadly divided into technical and operational complexity, and that for operational systems, the complexity of the operation is higher, while for framework systems, the technical complexity is higher. Addressing both levels of complexity poses considerable challenges.

The crowd-sourcing system that has been used previously is business logic, past, and, in fact, high complexity. In order to address this problem, we have begun to explore and practice-driven design (DDD), which has indeed brought some help, making the system less confusing. At the same time, I think that in this process, my own awareness of DDD has helped me in the subsequent division and design of the project system, as well as in its development.

Of course, DDD is not a silver bullet, and I'm not bragging about how good it is, but when it's learned, sometimes when it's designed and developed, it's a different way of thinking.

It turns out that it's not that easy for us to do business properly, and that it's good for people and for business if we can explore more and more, and bring in some good methods, ideas, or structures.
