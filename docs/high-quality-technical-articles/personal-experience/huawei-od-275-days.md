# The content of the OD

**With endless expectations, the fire rushes to China.**

Like HR who recruited him, he worked with his own staff.

> Managers allocate work according to your level of competence, gradually making it more difficult and more productive; for a six-month probationary period, you have a mentor, usually two Level-owned employees, mostly department bulls.

So, **there is no outsourcing that is based, process-based, non-technical** - I don't have to worry about this at all. You just need to find out what exactly the department/group is going to do, and you can accept it and think about the rest.

And one of the things that's really touching is that China is a giant with nearly 200,000 employees, and there's a lot of processes and systems inside. The benefits are that access to a range of processes ranging from the development, testing, to the release, transport, etc. of products from large companies, such as the submission of codes, will be given to you by experienced and internally certified bulls who can learn from their perspective when they are examined, as well as control over the whole product.

At the same time, however, individuals feel that this is not a good thing: a cumbersome process can lead to less efficient work, such as changing several lines of code, requiring a full CI (some of which take longer) and providing self-checking and VT reports.
